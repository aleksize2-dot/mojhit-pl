# Claude Haiku: Use Cases & Strengths

**What is Claude Haiku?**
Claude Haiku is Anthropic's fastest, most compact, and most cost-effective model in the Claude family. While Opus is the "heavyweight" for complex reasoning and Sonnet is the "balanced" middle ground, Haiku is built for **speed and high-volume processing**.

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Lightning Fast:** Near-instant responsiveness, reading dense documents or generating text in milliseconds.
- **Cost-Effective:** It is significantly cheaper than Sonnet or Opus (e.g., $0.25 input / $1.25 output per 1M tokens for Claude 3 Haiku).
- **Vision Capable:** Like the rest of the Claude 3 family, it can analyze images and documents.
- **Large Context Window:** It still supports the massive 200K token context window.

**Where it makes sense to use Claude Haiku:**

1. **Customer Support / Chatbots (High Volume):**
   - When you need a chatbot to answer FAQs instantly based on a company knowledge base.
   - **Why:** The latency is so low that the user feels they are chatting with a highly responsive system, and the cost won't bankrupt the company at scale.

2. **Data Extraction & Categorization:**
   - Parsing thousands of receipts, invoices, or unstructured emails to extract specific JSON data (e.g., "Find the date, amount, and vendor in these 10,000 PDFs").
   - **Why:** It's smart enough to find the data accurately, and fast/cheap enough to process thousands of documents in bulk.

3. **Moderation & Content Filtering:**
   - Scanning user-generated content (comments, reviews, forum posts) in real-time to flag toxicity, spam, or off-topic posts before they go live.
   - **Why:** Requires sub-second latency to not disrupt the user experience.

4. **"First Pass" Routing (Agentic Workflows):**
   - In a complex AI Agent system, Haiku acts as the "receptionist." It reads the user's prompt, decides what tools are needed or which specialized, expensive model (like Opus) should handle the actual work.
   - **Why:** You don't want to pay $15/1M tokens to an Opus model just to figure out that the user asked a basic question. Haiku does the cheap triage.

5. **UI/UX Interactions:**
   - Auto-completing forms, generating quick summaries of long articles for a "TL;DR" button, or suggesting quick reply options in a messaging app.

**When NOT to use Haiku:**
- Deep coding tasks, complex math, writing a novel, or nuanced emotional therapy (AI Psychologist). For these, you need the depth and nuance of Sonnet or Opus.
